Para-Ctrl: Intensity Controllable Style Transfer Speech Synthesis based on Codec Language Model


Abstract

In recent years, Text-to-Speech (TTS) technology has made significant advancements, with language model (LM)-based TTS systems emerging as the leading approach. These systems have shown remarkable capabilities in zero-shot speech synthesis, approaching human-level speech quality. However, challenges remain in style transfer, particularly with issues such as prosody averaging and speaker leakage. These limitations stem from conflicts in style information at varying levels of granularity provided by different prompts. To address these challenges, we propose Para-Ctrl, a robust LM-based TTS framework designed to enhance style transfer. Para-Ctrl introduces a frame-level Style Transfer Pitch Predictor to bolster frame-level speaker information and style representation. It also incorporates a Speaker Speaking Style Alignment module to improve style expressiveness while maintaining speaker identity, offering precise control over style intensity in synthesized speech. Experimental results demonstrate that Para-Ctrl effectively overcomes the limitations of existing methods, delivering superior style transfer performance and enabling fine-grained manipulation of style intensity.
